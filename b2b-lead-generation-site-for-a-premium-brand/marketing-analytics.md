# Marketing analytics — what I bring beyond the website

A short map of where data work compounds for a premium-water brand at your stage. Each block has the question it answers, what you receive, and what I need from you to start. Available in Spanish on request.

---

## 1. Market sizing & demand intelligence — *before launch*

**Question.** How big is the addressable market in Colombia, and which export markets are reachable from your port?

**What you get.** A short demand brief built from Statista, Euromonitor, DANE and ProColombia data — domestic premium-water sizing, channel breakdown (HoReCa, retail, fitness), and observed price points by channel. For export, ITC Trade Map and import-data screening identify destinations near you where premium-water demand outpaces local supply.

**What I need from you.** Port location, planned monthly capacity, target launch SKUs.

---

## 2. Competitive landscape & positioning

**Question.** Who do hotel, gym and restaurant buyers choose today, and at what price?

**What you get.** A short comparison of the brands actively serving Colombian B2B accounts, their on-table and wholesale prices where observable, and the positioning gaps your two-mode brand can occupy. Includes a one-page map your sales team can use to answer "why us?" in one line.

**What I need from you.** A handful of sample accounts you'd like to win.

---

## 3. Lead capture, CRM and pipeline — *at launch*

**Question.** Which sales channels work, which deals are stuck, and where do leads drop off?

**What you get.** Site form to CRM with source attribution, pipeline stages tuned to B2B water (first contact → sample → trial order → reorder cycle), and a weekly one-page dashboard: leads by source, conversion %, days to first order, average cases per active account. Works with HubSpot Free or Pipedrive on day one.

**What I need from you.** ~30 minutes to pick a CRM and grant access.

---

## 4. ERP to CRM bridge — *when production starts*

**Question.** Which accounts are reordering on schedule, and which are slipping?

**What you get.** Once the bottling line and its ERP are live, every printed label can become a "delivered" event in the CRM. From that you get revenue per account, reorder lag (the strongest churn signal in beverage), and stock-versus-demand balancing so service levels stay high without overproducing.

**What I need from you.** ERP access details once the machines arrive.

---

## 5. Campaign measurement

**Question.** Which marketing spend is paying back?

**What you get.** WhatsApp click tracking (each link tagged so you can see which page and CTA converts), paid-social cost per qualified B2B lead in your priority cities, and unique distributor codes so referrals are credited cleanly. Reporting is a weekly one-pager you can read in 60 seconds.

**What I need from you.** Direction on ad budget once you're ready to pilot paid.

---

## How this looks in practice

Setup for items 1–3 typically lands in two to four weeks and is mostly automated afterwards. Items 4 and 5 unlock as data lands. I report in plain language — one short weekly summary, deep dives only when something is worth investigating.
